We are hitting a big Kirby 3 milestone today with the first private single-language beta for our [supporters](https://getkirby.com/next). We also [started to answer questions about the new version](https://getkirby.com/blog/questions-about-kirby-3) on our blog and in our forum and [invite you to contribute your questions](https://forum.getkirby.com/t/kirby-3-ask-us-anything/10662/20).

## Kirby Enhanced

- [Simplemap](https://github.com/hdodov/kirby-simplemap-field) integrates Google Maps into the Panel, with features like localization, map styles, markers and more (free).

- [Mockmodule](https://github.com/plusForta/mockmodule) is a plugin for all Kirby users who use both the Modules and the Patterns plugins. It allows you to add static content for modules in the pattern lab (free).

- The [Kirby TOC plugin](https://github.com/jenstornell/kirby-toc) is an enhancement of the TOC plugin you probably know from the https://getkirby.com website with support for multiple heading levels (free).

<small>Always test third party plugins thoroughly before using them in production!</small>

## CSS Grid

- Rachel Andrew on [CSS Grid Level 2](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/2018/07/css-grid-2/) subgrids, currently under specification, which will solve some of the problems of nested grids. She also published the slides of her talk [Graduating to Grid](https://noti.st/rachelandrew/VibH0k/graduating-to-grid) on Notist.

- Laura Kalbag wrote [a short post about how CSS Grid](https://laurakalbag.com/gallery-grid-with-css-grid/)helped her to create a gallery grid on her photo blog.

- How can we use CSS Grid in Internet Explorer? Daniel Tonon explores this question in a 3-part series of posts. Part 1: [Debunking Common IE Grid Misconceptions](https://css-tricks.com/css-grid-in-ie-debunking-common-ie-grid-misconceptions/) and Part 2: [CSS Grid and the New Autoprefixer](https://css-tricks.com/css-grid-in-ie-css-grid-and-the-new-autoprefixer/) have already been published.

## GDPR

- With [Yett](https://github.com/snipsco/yett) you control the execution of (third party) scripts on your website. By putting it at the top of your website, you can intercept script calls to ask for consent from users before running analytics or other privacy related scripts, facilitating the implementation of GDPR compliance.

## A11y

- In [Don't Use the Placeholder Attribute](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/2018/06/placeholder-attribute/), Eric Bailey sums up the issues related to this attribute and why it's better to drop it.

- The Powermapper website contains a suite of [A11y testing tools](https://www.powermapper.com/tests), including [a list of HTML elements](https://www.powermapper.com/testss/screen-readers/elements/:) and their behavior in different screen readers.

- [Inclusive Design Principles](https://inclusivedesignprinciples.org) are 7 short "rules" addressing the key factors to keep in mind when designing for people with different disabilities. The principles are available as posters you can put up in your office as an everyday reminder.

- The [Accessibility Inspector](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Tools/Accessibility_inspector) developer tool, available in Firefox browsers since version 61, reads the information provided by the browser accessibility APIs and visualizes the accessibility tree of your website.

- Have you ever tried to surf the web with just a keyboard? If you haven't and want to get some insights from second hand experience, check out Chris Ashtons report about [a day on the web without a mouse](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/2018/07/web-with-just-a-keyboard/).
